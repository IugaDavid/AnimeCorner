Haikyu!! FINAL Anime Film Shows Rivals Squaring off in IMAX Movie Poster

Last month, it was announced that the decisive volleyball match between Karasuno and Nekoma would be shown on IMAX screens in Japan when the first Haikyu!! FINAL anime film opens. And while we can't wait to see everyone on both teams in action, the new poster for the IMAX release shows what everyone's really here to see: the face-off between Hinata and Kenma.

Toho Animation returns to produce the two Haikyu!! FINAL anime films with the animation continuing to be made by the team at Production I.G, including Susumu Mitsunaka as director and screenwriter, Takahiro Kishida as character designer and Takahiro Chiba as chief animation director. The first film, subtitled "Gomisuteba no Kessen,” is scheduled to be released in Japan on February 16, 2024.

Haikyu!! ran in Weekly Shonen Jump from February 20, 2012, to July 20, 2020, with all 402 chapters collected into 45 volumes. All the chapters are available to read in English on the Shonen Jump app.

Oi! Tonbo Golf Anime Releases New Trailer Introducing Character Voices

by: Mikikazu Komatsu

The official website for the forthcoming TV anime adaptation of Ken Kawasaki (story) and Yu Furusawa's (arts) golf-themed manga Oi! Tonbo has released a 90-second new trailer. The TV anime is set to premiere in Japan in April 2024.

The clip introduces the anime's main characters with their voices for the first time, including the protagonist Tonbo Oi played by Rika Hayashi and Iga Igarashi by Hiroki Touchi (Heiter in Frieren: Beyond Journey's End).

"Tonbo is just a straight kid. All the balls she shots break to the left and right, though! (laugh)." Hayashi said on the website. "The world in Tonbo's eyes is all fresh and full of excitement and thrill. You can't miss the free thinking and many golf scenes. I am very honored to be involved in this very wonderful work. I will do my best to play the role so that everyone who supports Oi! Tonbo will enjoy."

Luxury Merch Brand Unveils Its New Berserk Collaboration Line

by: Timothy Donohoo

Fans of Berserk can dress like the Black Swordsman himself thanks to a brand-new fashionable collaboration.

The most striking of these new Berserk items is the black leather jacket. The stylish biker jacket bears the Brand of Sacrifice symbol that's synonymous with protagonist Guts. This makes the Berserk jacket a bit more subtle, but fans will know exactly what the symbols on the clothing mean. However, the item will cost a pretty penny with an asking price of around US$260. The three Berserk-themed watches are based on Griffith, Guts' Berserker Armor and the Skull Night, with Guts' being the most visually imposing.

Two of these are US$260, although the Guts watch comes at a higher price point of $350. There's also a distressed scarf that evokes Guts' torn cloak, with the metallic pin recreating the Dragonslayer sword and the Berserker armor's design. Sold as a set, these items can be bought for $120. When combined into a single ensemble, Berserk fans who've shelled out the required cash can sport an outfit worthy of the beloved Kentaro Miura series.

Monkey D. Luffy Reviews Netflix's Live-Action One Piece

by: Chike Nwaenie

Monkey D. Luffy personally reviews Netflix's One Piece, as his English dub voice actor Colleen Clinkenbeard gives a glowing report of the series.

Colleen Clinkenbeard, the English dub voice actor for One Piece's Monkey D. Luffy in the anime, has now watched the Netflix live-action adaptation of the series, giving a glowing review of the latter.

In a post to X (formerly Twitter), Clinkenbeard praised the grand effort that went into condensing the story's "East Blue Saga" into just eight episodes. "Just finished @OnePieceNetflix and I really enjoyed it," she said. "It's hard to get all of that content into such a small number of episodes, but they managed it while keeping the heart of the story intact. So fun to watch all of the characters fall in love with Luffy! How could you not?"

Dragon Ball Super Delivers One of the Most Important Roasts of the Year in Chapter 101

by: Chike Nwaenie

Dragon Ball Super Chapter 101 again confirms for many fans why Vegeta and Goku's rivalry is among the best and most exciting in all of shonen.

Dragon Ball Super Chapter 101 confirms for many readers and franchise fans why Vegeta and Goku's rivalry is among the most engaging in shonen manga -- and how their dynamics have both grown over the years while remaining true to their roots.

Dragon Ball Super released Chapter 101, titled, "Carmine and Soldier #15," on Jan. 18, 2024. As Goku, Vegeta, Whis and Beerus exchange information on the latest events on Earth, with Gohan and Piccolo taking down Cell Max, Goku's awe leads him straight into a Vegeta roast. Goku regrets not knowing that his son was in action due to his duel with Vegeta, prompting the latter to reply, "Heh, heh... indeed. The duel... which... you lost to me." The exchange was notable to fans, with many highlighting Goku's put-out reaction as an example of how Super has truly put the two on an equal footing.

Kodansha Partners With Crunchyroll on Exclusive-Edition Initial D Manga Cover

by: Renee Senzatimore

Crunchyroll teams up with Japanese publisher Kodansha for an exclusive Volume 1 omnibus for Shuichi Shigeno's classic street racing manga, Initial D.

Crunchyroll and Kodansha are teaming up to release an exclusive manga omnibus for Initial D.

According to Crunchyroll's official site, Kodansha is republishing Shuichi Shigeno's classic racing manga with a series of new commemorative covers for each respective volume. The first omnibus, which compiles Vol. 1 and Vol. 2 of Initial D, features orange-hued foil-stamped artwork of the AE86 Trueno, the car that protagonist Takumi Fujiwara drives in the series. The new edition also includes an updated English translation. The omnibus is available now for pre-order via Crunchyroll's online shop. However, due to licensing/shipping restrictions, it can only be shipped to those residing in either the U.S. or Canada. Vol. 1 is scheduled to hit shelves on March 19, 2024.

Tite Kubo Determined to "Ensure His Vision" in Bleach's Final Anime Saga

by: Chike Nwaenie

Bleach manga creator Tite Kubo teases further involvement in the Thousand Year Blood War Season 3 to ensure his vision from the manga is fulfilled.

Bleach manga creator Tite Kubo has teased further personal involvement in the anime's production ahead of Season 3.

Via Reddit, a Q&A from Kubo's Klub Outside revealed the manga author said he will be more involved in the storyboarding process to ensure his vision is properly translated from manga to anime. A translation taken from X (formerly Twitter) reads, "I'm checking the settings and production of the anime graphics and storyboard for the third cour of the anime. I feel like I'm starting to get more involved/involved in the storyboard details of the episodes. Normally, when a manga is converted into anime format, the image or message of the manga event will vary slightly depending on the audience, so my current work on it is to fill in the discrepancies between the different scenes in detail."

Yen Press Reveals Its Top-Selling Manga and Novels of 2023

by: Chike Nwaenie

Popular publishing house Yen Press reveals its top-selling manga, manhwa, novels and debut titles for 2023, featuring popular works like Oshi no Ko.

Popular American publishing house Yen Press has released its official list of its best-selling manga, manhwa and novels for 2023, with some predicited winners but also perhaps a few surprises.

Yen Press revealed these highlights in a recent video posted to X (formerly Twitter). The platform published a total of 618 titles in 2023, with the top genres being fantasy, science-fiction, action-adventure, romance and horror, with Toilet-bound Hanako-kun being its best-selling manga series of the year. Meanwhile, Oshi no Ko., Vol. 1 was the best-selling individual volume of the year. "2023 was a big year for idols, villainesses, and stray dogs! It was also a big year for Yen Press, thanks to YOU!🥳," the post reads.
